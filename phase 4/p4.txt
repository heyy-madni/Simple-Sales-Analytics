Phase 4: Real Analytics

We don’t randomly write queries.

We answer business questions.

Step 1 — Validate Data (Do This First)

Before any analytics, run:

Count customers

Count products

Count orders

If counts look wrong, stop.

Garbage data → garbage analytics.

Step 2 — Revenue Calculation (Foundation)

Revenue per order = price × quantity

But price is in products table.
Quantity is in orders table.

So you must:

JOIN orders with products

Create computed column

This is your first test of relational thinking.

If your join is wrong, revenue is wrong.

Step 3 — Total Revenue

Once revenue per order works:

SUM(price × quantity)

Now you understand:
Row-level calculation → Aggregation

That’s the core of analytics.

Step 4 — Revenue Per Month

Now you group by time.

You will need:

Extract month from order_date

GROUP BY month

SUM revenue

This tests:

Date handling

Grouping logic

If dates were generated poorly, you’ll feel it here.

Step 5 — Top 5 Customers

Join:
orders → customers → products

Group by customer.
Order by SUM(revenue) DESC.
Limit 5.

This shows whether your dataset has meaningful distribution.

If all customers look equal, your data generation was too uniform.

Step 6 — Revenue by Category

Group by product category.

This tells you:
Is one category dominating?

Again, if results are flat, your data lacks structure.

Important Concept: SQL Does the Math

You do NOT:

Fetch rows

Loop in Python

Multiply manually

That’s inefficient and wrong approach.

Database is built for aggregation.

Python is for display.
